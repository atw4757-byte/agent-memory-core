# AMB v2.0-alpha results

## Headline

**Null result.** The 72 alpha runs produced scores that are invariant
across noise rates `{0.20, 0.30, 0.45, 0.60}` for every adapter. This
is not a code bug — it's a methodology finding: the `mini` scenario
(5 days, 4 queries, 7 timeline events) does not contain enough signal
to separate memory adapters under synthetic noise.

We report this openly rather than fabricate a ranking.

## Numbers

Mean across 3 seeds; identical at every noise rate in `{0.20, 0.30,
0.45, 0.60}`.

| Adapter | Mode | AUC | Quality@90 |
|---|---|---|---|
| `naive-append-only` | stock | 90.000 | 1.000 |
| `naive-append-only` | tuned | 90.000 | 1.000 |
| `langchain-buffer` | stock | 90.000 | 1.000 |
| `langchain-buffer` | tuned | 51.750 | 0.150 |
| `agent-memory-core` | stock | 33.075 | 0.350 |
| `agent-memory-core` | tuned | 33.075 | 0.350 |

(AUC = trapezoid integral of quality over days 0-90; not normalized.)

## Why this is a null result

1. With 4 queries and substring-containment matching, an adapter that
   concatenates its top-5 retrieved chunks wins any time one of those
   chunks contains the expected phrase. `naive-append-only` does
   exactly that, and on a 5-day scenario with unique phrases per query,
   the right chunk is always in its top-5.
2. Noise chunks have word overlap 0 with the queries (different
   templates) → they never displace matching chunks from top-5 under
   word-intersection scoring. Result: noise rate has no effect.
3. The `agent-memory-core` adapter uses embedding retrieval, whose
   recall on a 5-day scenario depends on the embedding model's luck
   with the specific phrases. Hence its lower score; it is not a
   capability ranking.
4. `langchain-buffer tuned` drops chunks older than 30 days — the mini
   scenario's authoritative events are all at days 0-4, so tuned mode
   purges the ground truth at day 30+ and collapses to 0.15. This is a
   real finding about the tuned policy on short scenarios, but it is
   not a production-relevant signal.

## What this means for the leaderboard

**No ordinal claim is published from v2.0-alpha.**

Per the [pre-registration](../../PREREGISTERED.md), the ordinal
prediction (`agent-memory-core (tuned) > agent-memory-core (stock) ≈
langchain (tuned) > langchain (stock) > naive`) required confirmation
across ≥3 of 4 noise rates. The data does not support that claim
because the data does not differentiate at any noise rate.

**This counts as "ordinal prediction NOT CONFIRMED."** Pre-registration
worked as intended: we are recording a miss rather than reinterpreting
the formula.

## What ships in v2.0-alpha

- **Working harness + 3 adapters** with stock/tuned modes.
- **Frozen methodology** (pre-registered formula, SHA-pinned impl).
- **72-cell sensitivity grid** rendered as a chart — visibly flat,
  which is itself informative.
- **Reproducible**: `python -m benchmark.amb_v2.run_all --scenarios
  benchmark/amb_v2/scenarios --out-dir results/alpha-v2.0 ...`
  completes in ~40s on Bosgame (Ryzen, macOS).

## What's needed for v2.0.1

Per the [deferred items in the spec][spec], v2.0.1 will add:

1. **3 held-out scenarios** generated by Gemini 2.5 Pro from the
   public spec only (driver at `scripts/generate_held_out.py`).
   Each held-out will be 20-30 timeline events over 90 days,
   ≥2 contradictions, ≥1 credential, ≥8 queries — enough signal to
   drive real ordinal separation.
2. **LlamaIndex and Mem0 adapters**, smoke-tested now, integrated in
   v2.0.1.
3. **Real-data validation track** (D12): reproduce the v2.0.1 top-3
   result on a held-out slice of a real conversational dataset before
   any public leaderboard claim.
